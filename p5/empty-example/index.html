Simulation of Solar Sails by Justin Yuen

Physics 454 - Professor Hedberg

Description

The surface area of the sail directly increases how much force sunlight can apply onto the sail.

Here, the solar sails are represented by squares.

Here, assume the bigger sail's area is twice of the smaller sail. (Not drawn to scale)

Consequently, the bigger sail's acceleration is twice of the smaller sail.

The blue lines above the square sails represent the magnitudes of the sail's acceleration.

Controls

Simply click either the screen or the "Reset" button to restart the simulation.
